The financial impact from a cybersecurity attack can be downright detrimental to SMBs. The average cost of a data breach in North America is $117,000 (Kaspersky 2016).

According to a Ponemon Institute research study from 2017, cybersecurity attacks have targeted SMBs more in the past 12 month, with an increase from 55 percent to 61 percent of respondents (Ponemon 2017).

Cybersecurity insurance helps companies recover from any sort of data loss. Even though prices and exclusions (a risk not covered by an insurance policy) vary widely between insurers, there are still common areas that are covered with typical cybersecurity insurance requirements. These typical requirements are incident response expense coverage, funds transfer coverage, personal information protection, and incident response consultation. According to a 2017 FICO survey, 50% of US executives surveyed said their firm has no cybersecurity insurance, and 27% of US executives said their firms have no plans to take out cybersecurity insurance (PR Newswire 2017). In today’s world, it is vital for a company to maintain strong cybersecurity insurance.
